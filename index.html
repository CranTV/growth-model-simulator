Model Parameters
A Technology (TFP) 1.00
Shifts curve
A↑ raises output at every capital level simultaneously — a vertical curve shift.
Total Factor Productivity (TFP) — captures technology, institutional quality, and human capital. Because A is a multiplicative shifter, it lifts the entire production function.A↑ → y = Akα shifts up → curve shift🌍 China's 1978 Open Door reforms: technology inflows constituted a large positive A shock, shifting the production frontier upward.
α Capital Share 0.33
Reshapes curve
Controls curvature. α = 1 → straight 45° line (full automation).
Capital's Output Elasticity (α) — also capital's share of national income. At α = 0.33, capital earns one-third of every unit of output.α → 1 → Y = AK (AK model, no diminishing returns)🤖 Rising automation pushes α upward, compressing the labour share (1−α) and flattening the curve toward linearity.
K Capital 4.0
Moves dot along curve
K↑ raises K/L — the dot slides right along the current curve. The curve does not shift.
Physical Capital (K) — machinery, infrastructure, equipment. An increase in K with L held constant deepens capital per worker, moving the dot rightward subject to diminishing returns.K↑ (L fixed) → k = K/L↑ → dot moves right⚙️ Installing additional machinery is capital deepening — movement along the curve, not a shift.
L Labour 2.0
Moves dot along curve
L↑ reduces K/L (capital dilution) — the dot slides left along the current curve.
Labour Force (L) — a growing workforce spreads capital more thinly. A shrinking workforce raises k and moves the dot rightward.L↑ (K fixed) → k↓ → dot moves leftL↓ (ageing) → k↑ → dot moves right👴 China's working-age population peaked circa 2015. L↓ → K/L↑ → LP rises, but aggregate GDP decelerates.
Full Automation Zone — α = 0.33
The production curve is approaching a 45° line. Diminishing returns to capital are fading; labour's income share (1−α) approaches zero. This corresponds to the AK growth model.

Analytical Commentary
Adjust any parameter to generate commentary. The baseline curve (green) is permanently fixed at A = 1.50, α = 0.33, K = 5.0, L = 2.0.

Modifying A will produce a vertical shift of the current curve. Modifying K or L will produce movement along the current curve. Modifying α will reshape the curvature of the current curve.
Glossary of Terms
All variables and concepts used in this simulation
Y
Output / GDP
Output
Total value of goods and services produced. Determined by A, K, and L. Expressed per worker on the y-axis.
Y = AKαL1−α → y = Y/L = Akα
📈 y is the y-axis. Higher y = higher labour productivity = higher long-run living standards.
A
TFP / Technology
Technology
Captures everything that raises output beyond raw K and L: technology, institutions, human capital, governance. A↑ shifts the entire curve upward. The only driver of sustained per-worker growth.
A = Y / (KαL1−α) — Solow Residual
🔬 Solow (1957): approximately seven-eighths of US growth 1909–1949 was attributable to A, not K or L accumulation.
K
Physical Capital
Capital
Productive stock of machinery, infrastructure, and equipment. K↑ with L held constant raises k = K/L — capital deepening — moving the dot rightward subject to diminishing returns.
k = K/L (x-axis) | ∂²y/∂k² < 0
🏭 “More machines” (K↑) = dot moves right. “Better machines” (A↑) = current curve shifts up.
L
Labour Force
Labour
Number of workers. L↑ dilutes the capital stock, reducing k and moving the dot left. L↓ raises k and moves the dot right, lifting labour productivity even as total GDP growth moderates.
L↑ → k↓ → dot left | L↓ → k↑ → dot right
👴 China: L↓ → K/L↑ → LP rises, but %ΔY = %ΔLP + %ΔL declines.
α
Capital Share of Income
Production
Capital's output elasticity and income share. Controls curvature. At α = 0.33, capital earns one-third of output. As α → 1, the curve straightens to a 45° line — the AK model.
α ≈ 0.33 (empirical) | α = 1 → Y = AK
🤖 Automation pushes α upward: robots substitute for labour, raising the capital share.
H
Human Capital
Technology
Worker skills, education, and health. In the extended model, H folds into A = A'H(1−α). Improved education therefore constitutes an A shock — a curve shift, not movement along.
Y = A'Kα(HL)1−α → A = A'H1−α
🏫 Education and training raise H → treated as A↑ in this diagram → current curve shifts up.
DMR
Diminishing Marginal Returns
Concept
Each additional unit of K/L adds progressively less Y/L — the fundamental source of the curve's concavity. Applies to K and L individually, not to A. This is why capital accumulation alone cannot sustain long-run per-worker growth.
∂y/∂k > 0 and ∂²y/∂k² < 0
🏗 First machine: large gain. One hundredth machine in a crowded facility: marginal gain.
%ΔY
GDP Growth Decomposition
Growth
GDP growth equals Labour Productivity growth plus Labour force growth. LP grows via A↑ (curve shift) or k↑ (movement along). Only sustained A growth maintains LP growth as DMR sets in on capital deepening.
%ΔY = %ΔLP + %ΔL | LP = Y/L = Akα
📉 China's slowdown reflects simultaneous deceleration in LP growth and L growth.
